Product vs Project

In software development, it is important to understand the difference between a Product and a Project.

What is a Project?

A Project is a software application developed to meet the specific requirements of a single customer or client. It is usually tailored and customized for individual needs and is delivered for that particular customer.

What is a Product?

A Product is a software application developed for multiple customers or a wider market. It is designed to meet the general needs of many users and is usually released to the public or multiple organizations.

Key Differences

  • Customer Focus: Project serves a specific client; Product is designed for multiple customers.
  • Customization: Project is highly customized; Product is more standardized.
  • Scope: Projects have defined scope per customer needs; Products have broader, evolving scope to fit a larger market.
  • Development Approach: Projects usually have fixed timelines and specifications; Products may have continuous development and upgrades.
Tip: Understanding whether you are working on a product or project affects the development, testing approach, and delivery strategy.
