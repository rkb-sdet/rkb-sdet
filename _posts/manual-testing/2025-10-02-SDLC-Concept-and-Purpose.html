SDLC Concept and Purpose

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is a structured and systematic process used by software development teams to design, develop, test, and maintain high-quality software. It provides a clear framework of phases and activities, ensuring that the final product meets the needs of customers and stakeholders.

What is SDLC?

SDLC defines the complete journey of software developmentâ€”from initial planning and requirements gathering through design, coding, testing, deployment, and ultimately maintenance and retirement. By following SDLC processes, teams can efficiently build software that is cost-effective, reliable, and of high quality.

Purpose of SDLC

  • Deliver High-Quality Software: SDLC aims to produce software that is robust, maintainable, and meets user requirements.
  • Minimize Risks: Structured planning and well-defined stages help reduce project risks and avoid expensive mistakes.
  • Improve Efficiency: By dividing work into discernible phases, SDLC allows better management of time, cost, and resources.
  • Increase Visibility: All stakeholders gain clarity about the goals, processes, and expectations at every development stage.
  • Facilitate Teamwork: SDLC enhances collaboration between developers, testers, customers, and management by providing clear deliverables at each stage.
  • Continuous Improvement: The iterative nature of SDLC encourages regular feedback, adjustment, and ongoing improvements.
Tip: SDLC framework supports software delivery on schedule, within budget, and with desired quality, making it an essential practice for successful projects.
