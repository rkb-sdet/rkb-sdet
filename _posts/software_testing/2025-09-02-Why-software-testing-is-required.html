1. Buggy Software with Low Quality

  • If no testing is done, software may be released with defects (bugs).

  • Impact: End-users face broken features → dissatisfaction.

  • Example:

    • An e-commerce site’s “Add to Cart” button doesn’t work → user cannot complete purchase.

  • Core Idea: Unchecked bugs = poor quality product in the market.

2. Loss of Business Reputation

  • Reason: Customers lose trust in the brand if they face issues.

  • Example:

    • A user orders 1 item but, due to a defect, 10 items are ordered and charged.

    • Customer experiences inconvenience and frustration.

  • Result:

    • Negative feedback spreads.

    • Customers move to competitors (Amazon, Flipkart, etc.).

  • Key Insight: Bad first impression = long-term damage.

3. Legal Issues – Customer Lawsuits

  • Situation: Defective products/services may cause harm or violate expectations.

  • Example from another industry:

    • Cosmetic cream claims skin improvement but causes rashes.

    • Customer files a case, wins → company pays large compensation.

  • Software parallel:

    • Faulty banking software leads to wrong transactions.

  • Impact: Financial loss + legal damage.

4. Drop in Sales → Revenue Loss

  • Reason: Defects stop customers from completing purchases.

  • Example:

    • User fills checkout form but can’t proceed due to a bug.

  • Impact:

    • Sales decline.

    • Company revenue decreases.

  • Flow Diagram:

    Defects → Purchase Failure → Lost Sales → Revenue Loss

5. Severe Cases – Loss of Life

  • When: Software controls critical systems (medical, aviation, defense).

  • Examples:

    1. Medical Equipment:

      • Surgery machine is programmed to operate on the left brain but, due to a bug, operates on the right → fatal consequences.

    2. Aviation:

      • Airplane autopilot malfunction causes it to deviate from course → crash.

  • Impact:

    • Human fatalities.

    • Massive lawsuits.

    • Irreversible brand damage.

6. Summary Table

7. Key Takeaway

Testing is essential to ensure software meets quality, safety, and user expectations before release.
Without it, businesses face:

  • Quality degradation

  • Reputation loss

  • Legal and financial risks

  • Potential human casualties
